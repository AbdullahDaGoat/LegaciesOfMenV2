By the Numbers

In 2021, 1.4 million suicide attempts occurred

1.4 Million Suicide Attempts

In 2021, the National Survey on Drug Use and Health reported that an estimated 1.4 million adults in the United States attempted suicide. This highlights the significant mental health challenge facing our society and the need for improved prevention and support services.

Of those attempts, 48,183 died

48,183 Deaths by Suicide

In the same year, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reported that 48,183 individuals in the United States died by suicide. This tragic statistic underscores the urgent need for improved mental health resources and support systems to prevent these devastating losses.

And about 39,000 were men

39,000 Male Suicide Deaths

The CDC data shows that approximately 39,000 of the 48,183 suicide deaths in 2021 were among men. This highlights the disproportionate impact of suicide on the male population and the need for targeted mental health support and education programs for men.

94% of People believe Suicide is Preventable, and they're right

94% Believe Suicide is Preventable

According to a survey by the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention, 94% of respondents believe that suicide is preventable with the right support and resources. This highlights the public's understanding of the importance of suicide prevention efforts and the need for continued investment in mental health services and education.

Source: American Foundation for Suicide Prevention
